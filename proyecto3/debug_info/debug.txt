<p><b>Beverly Elaine Aadland</b> (September 16, 1942 – January 5, 2010) was an American film actress.<sup id="cite_ref-obituary_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She appeared in films including <i><a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_(1958_film)" title="South Pacific (1958 film)">South Pacific</a></i>. As a teenager, she co-starred in the <a href="/wiki/Errol_Flynn" title="Errol Flynn">Errol Flynn</a> film<i> <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Rebel_Girls" title="Cuban Rebel Girls">Cuban Rebel Girls</a></i>, and had a relationship with him.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beverly_Aadland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aadland was born in the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. She entered show business as a child, appearing in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Salesman_(1951_film)" title="Death of a Salesman (1951 film)">Death of a Salesman</a></i> (1951).<sup id="cite_ref-opa_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-opa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beverly_Aadland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beverly Elaine Aadland was 17 at the time she was with actor <a href="/wiki/Erol_Flynn" class="mw-redirect" title="Erol Flynn">Errol Flynn</a> when he died of a heart attack on October 14, 1959, in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> at the age of 50.<sup id="cite_ref-obituary_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1961, Aadland's mother, Florence Aadland, alleged in the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Love" title="The Big Love">The Big Love</a></i> that actor Flynn had a sexual relationship with her daughter starting at age 15, yet there is also speculation he was led to believe she was 18.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The book would be turned into a one-woman Broadway show starring <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Ullman" title="Tracey Ullman">Tracey Ullman</a> as Florence. The memoir was reissued in 2018 by Spurl Editions.<sup id="cite_ref-richards_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-richards-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Beverly Aadland gave an account of her relationship with Flynn in <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> in 1988, confirming that she had had a sexual relationship with Flynn in her teens.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Her relationship with Flynn was the subject of the 2013 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_of_Robin_Hood" title="The Last of Robin Hood">The Last of Robin Hood</a></i>, in which Aadland was played by <a href="/wiki/Dakota_Fanning" title="Dakota Fanning">Dakota Fanning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beverly_Aadland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1960, William Stanciu, her then boyfriend, died in her apartment after being shot in a struggle between the two.<sup id="cite_ref-obituary_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> That event led to her being a <a href="/wiki/Ward_(law)" title="Ward (law)">ward</a> of the court for the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-opa_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-opa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aadland was married and divorced twice before she married Ronald Fisher in the late 1960s. The couple had a daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-obituary_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beverly Aadland Fisher died on January 5, 2010, at the <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_Community_Hospital" title="Lancaster Community Hospital">Lancaster Community Hospital</a> from complications of <a href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a> and congestive <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure" title="Heart failure">heart failure</a>. She was 67 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-obituary_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obituary-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
